# Common Phrases and Translations

## Purpose

SPECTRE Task Force often operates in multilingual environments, whether during joint operations or in contact with civilians or enemy forces. This reference provides tactical phrases and everyday commands in multiple languages to support roleplay, immersion, and operational clarity.

## Supported Languages

- English (base)
- Spanish
- Russian
- Arabic
- German
- Chinese (Simplified with Pinyin)

## Application

This document is organized into categories for:

- Tactical commands (e.g., "Contact front!", "Cover me!", "Frag out!")
- Interaction with civilians or hostiles (e.g., "Stop!", "Hands up!", "We won’t hurt you.")
- Non-tactical dialogue (e.g., greetings, requests for help)

Each phrase includes:

- English base phrase
- Translated form
- Approximate pronunciation (transliteration)

> 📘 Note: Pronunciations are intended to be readable by English speakers and may not be linguistically perfect.

## Usage Guidance

- Use tactical phrases when playing missions involving multilingual NPCs or joint forces.
- Brief teammates before missions that include language-based roleplay.
- Stick to critical or rehearsed phrases to avoid confusion.
- Consider assigning a squad member with stronger pronunciation for contact roles.
